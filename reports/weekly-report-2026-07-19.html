Analyst Notes
Ecommerce & Conversion
Working
Average order value is tracking above both last year and forecast at $156, which is softening the blow of fewer transactions overall.
Store sales more than doubled last year to $5,717, suggesting in-person engagement is genuinely growing rather than just recovering.
Needs Attention
Conversion rate dropped to 2.41% against a 3.0% forecast despite sessions being up 7.3%, meaning more traffic is arriving but fewer people are buying — this is the core issue this week.
Email is only driving 4% of sessions, which is low relative to its potential as a warm, owned channel that typically converts well above site average.
This week: Audit the on-site experience for the top landing pages driven by paid social traffic to identify any friction points that may be suppressing conversion rate this week.
Paid Media
Working
Overall return on ad spend of 4.2x is still comfortably ahead of the 3.0x forecast, so paid media is profitable even with the efficiency softening versus last year.
Cost per order at $38 is well below the $65 forecast, meaning the business is acquiring customers for significantly less than planned.
Needs Attention
Meta is taking the lion's share of spend at $14,395 and driving 41% of all sessions via paid social, but the site-wide conversion rate decline suggests this traffic may be less purchase-ready than last year's mix.
Google spend has jumped 51% versus last year to $3,540 spread across three campaigns, and with paid search delivering only 33 sessions it is worth checking whether budget is being efficiently allocated across Shopping and Performance Max.
This week: Review the audience and creative mix on Meta campaigns this week to assess whether the paid social traffic quality has shifted, given sessions are up but conversion rate is meaningfully down.
Retention & Revenue
Working
The fast shipping email on 16 July delivered a 52.3% open rate and $631 in revenue, showing that practical, timely messaging resonates with the list.
New customer share came in at 68.8% against a 65% forecast, which is healthy for brand growth and suggests paid acquisition is bringing in genuinely new buyers.
Needs Attention
The hiker survey email generated zero revenue despite a solid 42.2% open rate, which points to a disconnect between the email's goal and any purchase prompt — a missed opportunity given The Hiker is performing well in units sold.
Return customer share is down significantly year on year, sitting at 31.2% versus 64% last year, which warrants a closer look at whether post-purchase flows are doing enough to bring buyers back.
This week: Schedule a dedicated send to existing customers this week with a clear product or offer focus, specifically to rebuild the returning customer purchase rate that has softened sharply versus last year.
