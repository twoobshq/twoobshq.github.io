Analyst Notes
Ecommerce & Conversion
Working
Conversion rate hit 3.08% against a 3.0% forecast, and online sales of $104,678 are up 234% on last year, driven by paid social bringing in 43% of all sessions and clearly converting well once on site.
New customers made up 66% of orders, just ahead of the 65% forecast, which suggests the current creative and offer mix is reaching fresh audiences effectively.
Needs Attention
Email drove only 6% of sessions despite being a proven owned channel, and with just two campaigns sent this week both focused on a single promotion, there is room to do more work here.
The Hiker has only 15.1 weeks of stock cover at current sell-through pace, making it the tightest inventory position in the range and worth watching closely if demand holds.
This week: Check stock replenishment timelines for The Hiker this week given it has the lowest cover in the range at 15 weeks.
Paid Media
Working
Return on ad spend came in at 5.5x against a 3.0x forecast, and cost per order dropped to $27 versus a $65 forecast, meaning Meta's $17,058 spend this week was exceptionally efficient.
Google's Shopping campaign and Performance Max are doing their job as supporting channels, together accounting for $1,891 in spend at a comparatively low cost given the overall blended efficiency.
Needs Attention
Google's Paid Search campaign spent almost nothing at $29 for the week, which may simply reflect the account setup but is worth confirming it is not inadvertently paused or underfunded.
Meta is carrying 90% of total paid spend, so while results are strong now, the business is heavily exposed if Meta performance shifts — a small increase in Google budget would add useful diversification.
This week: Check the Google Paid Search campaign this week to confirm it is active and budgeted appropriately, or close it deliberately if Shopping and Performance Max are the intended focus.
Retention & Revenue
Working
Both Final Hours Free Shipping emails posted open rates above 40%, which is a strong result and shows the subject line and send timing landed well with the list.
Average order value online reached $151 against a $140 forecast, suggesting returning customers in particular are buying more per visit, likely helped by the free shipping incentive nudging them to add an extra item.
Needs Attention
Only two campaigns went out this week and both carried the same promotional message, so outside of the free shipping push there was no content, editorial, or product-led reason for customers to engage with the brand.
Returns jumped to 26 units and $3,219 in value compared to 9 units and $1,364 last year — worth a quick look at which products or sizes are coming back most to catch any fit or quality pattern early.
This week: Pull the returns data by product and size this week to identify whether any single style is driving the increase before it becomes a larger issue.
