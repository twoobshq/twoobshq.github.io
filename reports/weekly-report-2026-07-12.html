Analyst Notes
Ecommerce & Conversion
Working
Paid social is carrying the traffic load at 43% of sessions and, combined with a conversion rate of 3.07% beating forecast, it is translating visits into orders efficiently.
The store is also pulling its weight with $7,226 in net sales and an average transaction value of $147, up strongly on last year's $124.
Needs Attention
Email is only driving 6% of sessions, which is low relative to its potential given the strong open rates seen in campaigns this week — there is headroom to send more.
Organic social delivered just 259 sessions (1% of traffic), suggesting that owned social content is not converting curiosity into clicks the way paid social is.
This week: Schedule at least two additional email sends this week to capitalise on the audience already engaged at 40%-plus open rates and close the gap between email's potential and its current 6% session share.
Paid Media
Working
Meta is the clear engine this week, spending $17,057 and delivering a return on ad spend of 5.5x against a forecast of 3.0x, with a cost per order of $27 versus a forecast of $65 — that is exceptional efficiency.
The TWOOBS AU Shopping campaign is the top Google spender at $817 and, alongside the BFF and BFF+ Shopping campaign, is supporting the two best-selling products where stock cover is healthy enough to handle the volume.
Needs Attention
Google's Paid Search campaign spent almost nothing this week at $29, which likely means it has little to no budget or targeting in place — worth checking whether this is intentional.
The Performance Max campaign is spending $717 but its contribution relative to the Shopping campaigns is unclear; given how well Meta is performing, it is worth confirming Google budgets are allocated where they are actually working.
This week: Review the Google Paid Search campaign settings this week to confirm whether the near-zero spend is a deliberate choice or a budget or targeting issue that needs fixing.
Retention & Revenue
Working
The Final Hours Free Shipping email on 12 July generated $1,794 in revenue with a 40.9% open rate, showing that urgency-led offers are landing well with the existing customer base.
Average order value online hit $151 against a forecast of $140 and last year's $117, which points to customers buying more per visit — likely helped by the BFF+ driving strong attachment.
Needs Attention
The NAIDOC week donation resend generated only $75 in revenue despite a solid 40.2% open rate, suggesting the audience engaged but did not convert — the message or landing experience may need a clearer purchase prompt.
New customers are at 66% of orders, which is healthy for acquisition, but with only two campaigns sent this week there is little structured activity to bring returning customers back and lift that repeat purchase rate.
This week: Build a short returning-customer flow this week — even a single targeted email to past buyers who have not purchased in 60-plus days — to balance the acquisition-heavy mix and protect long-term lifetime value.
