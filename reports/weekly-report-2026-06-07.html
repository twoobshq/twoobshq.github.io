Analyst Notes
Ecommerce & Conversion
Working
Online sales of $86,410 are up 168% on last year, driven by a big jump in orders (575 vs 203 last year) and a doubling of sessions to 23,114.
New customers made up 68% of orders this week, ahead of the 65% forecast, which shows the brand is pulling in fresh audiences effectively.
Needs Attention
Conversion rate came in at 2.49%, below the 3.0% forecast — there is real upside here given how much traffic the site is receiving.
Returns have jumped to 32 units ($4,442) compared to 8 units ($1,237) last year, with the return rate rising from 3.1% to 4.3%.
This week: Review the top returning products this week to identify whether fit, sizing messaging, or product photography could be improved to bring the return rate back closer to last year's level.
Paid Media
Working
Return on ad spend of 4.2x is well above both last year (2.6x) and the forecast (3.0x), meaning every dollar spent on Meta is working harder than expected.
Cost per order dropped to $36 versus $61 last year and a forecast of $65, so the team is acquiring customers much more efficiently this week.
Needs Attention
Meta spend of $20,764 is up 69% on last year, so it is worth confirming that the current creative and audience mix can sustain this efficiency as spend scales.
Google remains unavailable this week, which means all paid traffic is relying on a single channel — any Meta disruption would have an outsized impact.
This week: Document which Meta creatives or audiences are driving the strong return on ad spend this week so the team can double down on what is working before spend levels increase further.
Retention & Revenue
Working
The BFF+ launched strongly with 253 units sold and 9.2 weeks of stock cover, making it the top product this week and a clear driver of new customer growth.
Average order value of $150 online is tracking above the $140 forecast, which is a solid result even if it sits slightly below last year's $159.
Needs Attention
Sockys stock cover is down to 12 weeks with 28 units sold last week, so this accessory line could become a gap in the range sooner than expected.
Protecty Stuff sold only 36 units against 132.8 weeks of stock on hand — that is a significant overstock position that is worth addressing soon.
This week: Set up a targeted email or on-site promotion this week to move Protecty Stuff, pairing it with the BFF+ or BFF as a bundle to reduce excess stock while adding value for new customers.
