Analyst Notes
Ecommerce & Conversion
Working
Average order value is running at $155 against a forecast of $140, suggesting customers are either trading up or adding more to their carts — this is the main driver behind online sales beating last year by 38% on only 2% more sessions.
New customers made up 64% of online orders this week, right on forecast and a significant jump from 43.5% last year, which points to paid social doing real acquisition work rather than just retargeting existing buyers.
Needs Attention
Conversion rate came in at 2.56% against a forecast of 3.0%, meaning there is a meaningful gap between the traffic arriving and the purchases completing — with paid social driving 42% of sessions, even a small improvement here would add materially to revenue.
Organic social delivered just 1% of sessions (225) while paid social carried 42%, so the brand is heavily reliant on spend to drive discovery with very little organic social momentum behind it.
This week: Run a quick audit of the top landing pages receiving paid social traffic this week to identify any friction points — slow load time, unclear sizing, or weak calls to action — that could be suppressing the conversion rate below forecast.
Paid Media
Working
Return on ad spend hit 5.2x against a forecast of 3.0x and last year's 3.6x, while cost per order dropped to $30 versus a $65 forecast, meaning the paid media engine is delivering exceptional efficiency this week with less total spend than last year.
The BFF+ Shopping campaign is pulling weight given the product moved 207 units this week compared to just 1 unit in the same week last year, suggesting the campaign is successfully introducing a relatively new hero product to a broad audience.
Needs Attention
Paid Search received only $23 in sessions as a channel this week, which is negligibly small, and it is worth confirming whether branded search campaigns are active and capturing intent from customers already looking for TWOOBS by name.
Google's total spend of $1,608 is split across three campaigns with the Performance Max campaign taking the largest share at $695 — without visibility into which campaign is converting, budget could be sitting in lower-performing placements without being noticed.
This week: Pull a conversion breakdown by Google campaign for the week to confirm whether the Performance Max or the Shopping campaigns are driving the orders, then shift budget toward whichever is delivering the lower cost per order.
Retention & Revenue
Working
The Loyalty relaunch email for the Pal Tier achieved a 45.6% open rate, which is a strong signal that the existing customer base is engaged with the programme and curious about what it offers — a good foundation to build the next tier reveal on.
Average order value of $155 is tracking $35 above forecast across both new and returning customers, suggesting that either product mix, bundling, or the loyalty programme itself is encouraging shoppers to spend more per visit.
Needs Attention
Both email campaigns this week generated very modest revenue — $218 and $163 respectively — despite healthy open rates, which suggests the emails are being read but not converting to purchases, pointing to a disconnect between the message and a compelling reason to buy now.
Returns value rose to $3,260 this week from $1,974 last year, with the return rate ticking up to 3.6% from 3.1% — worth monitoring whether this is concentrated in a specific product or size, particularly given the BFF+ is a new volume driver with no last-year comparison.
This week: Review the click-through and purchase data for both email sends to identify whether the offer, the product featured, or the landing page is the weak link, then adjust the next campaign to include a clearer single call to action tied to a time-limited incentive.
