Analyst Notes
Ecommerce & Conversion
Working
The physical store had a strong week, more than doubling last year's sales to $6,207 on 41 transactions, which suggests in-person demand is genuinely building.
Average order value is tracking above forecast at $148 versus the $140 target, and new customer acquisition at 70.7% is ahead of the 65% forecast, so the customers coming through are spending well and skewing fresh.
Needs Attention
Online conversion rate fell to 2.30% against a 3.0% forecast and last year's 5.00%, meaning a large share of the 20,803 sessions this week left without buying — the traffic volume isn't the main problem, the conversion is.
Email drove only 990 sessions (5% of total traffic) and paid social dominated at 46%, which means the owned-channel mix is heavily skewed toward paid and leaves the business exposed if Meta costs rise further.
This week: Run a quick audit of the on-site experience — landing pages, page speed, and the checkout flow — to identify any friction points that could explain the gap between traffic and conversion this week.
Paid Media
Working
Return on ad spend of 3.4x is ahead of the 3.0x forecast despite a significant increase in total spend to $20,960, so the paid mix is at least meeting its efficiency target for the week.
Cost per order came in at $44 against a forecast of $65, which means the business is acquiring customers more cheaply than planned even as spend has grown year on year.
Needs Attention
Meta is absorbing $18,923 of the $20,960 total paid budget — a 27% increase on last year — while Google's four campaigns share just $2,036, so almost all paid risk sits with one platform.
The Google Shopping and Performance Max campaigns are each spending under $730 with no campaign-level revenue reported, making it difficult to assess which is actually contributing to orders versus burning budget.
This week: Pull campaign-level conversion data for all four Google campaigns this week and pause or consolidate any that have not driven at least one order, then redirect that budget toward the best-performing Meta creative.
Retention & Revenue
Working
Average order value at $148 is above both last year ($116) and the forecast ($140), suggesting returning customers in particular are buying more per visit when they do convert.
The BFF+ is the top-selling product at 184 units with no prior year comparison, indicating new product launches are successfully drawing buyers back or attracting new ones at healthy volumes.
Needs Attention
The only email campaign sent this week — the Golden Ticket Competition on 1 June — generated zero revenue with a 7.8% open rate, which is well below typical engagement benchmarks and suggests the audience either missed the send or the offer did not motivate a purchase.
Returns have jumped to 4.9% of sales value (37 units, $4,116) compared to just 1.0% last year, which warrants a closer look at whether a specific product, size, or channel is driving the increase before it compounds further.
This week: Send a focused revenue-driving email this week — such as a product highlight or limited offer on the BFF+ — to the segment that has not purchased in the last 60 days, and monitor open and purchase rates closely given the low engagement from last week's send.
