Analyst Notes
Ecommerce & Conversion
Working
Average order value is running at $145 against a forecast of $140, and the store is having a standout week with sales up 325% on last year, suggesting the physical retail experience is resonating well.
The new customer mix at 63.5% is almost exactly on the 65% forecast, meaning acquisition efforts are pulling in fresh buyers at the right rate.
Needs Attention
Total online sessions are down 43% on last year and conversion rate has slipped to 2.76% against a 3.0% forecast, so fewer people are arriving and a slightly smaller share of them are buying — both levers need attention at once.
Returns have jumped sharply to 7.9% of sales (56 units, $7,036) compared to just 1.6% last year, which is worth investigating quickly to understand whether it is a fit issue, a product expectation gap, or a fulfilment problem.
This week: Pull the returns data by product and reason code this week to identify whether one style or size is driving the spike, and flag to the product team if a pattern emerges.
Paid Media
Working
Google Ads is delivering an exceptional return on ad spend of 39.6x at a cost per order of just $4, well ahead of both last year and the $65 forecast, meaning every dollar of paid search and shopping spend is working extremely hard right now.
The Performance Max shoes campaign and the standard Shopping campaign together account for the bulk of spend and are likely driving the efficiency, given how lean the total outlay is at $1,808 for the week.
Needs Attention
Total paid media spend is down 43% on last year with no Meta activity at all, which means the brand is almost entirely reliant on Google to do the paid acquisition work — that concentration is a risk if performance shifts.
With sessions down 43% year on year, the absence of Meta is almost certainly a contributing factor to the traffic shortfall, and the strong Google return on ad spend suggests there is headroom to reinvest profitably.
This week: Reactivate at least one Meta campaign this week — even a modest retargeting or prospecting test — to begin rebuilding paid social as a traffic source and reduce reliance on Google alone.
Retention & Revenue
Working
The Golden Ticket eDM leak sent on 17 June achieved a remarkable 63.9% open rate, showing that teaser-style subject lines and campaign anticipation are a strong hook for this audience.
The Last Chance Golden Ticket send on 21 June converted well at $3,052 revenue with a 41.6% open rate, confirming that urgency-based follow-up emails are an effective way to close out a campaign window.
Needs Attention
Email is driving only 10% of sessions this week, which is reasonable in isolation but worth monitoring given that overall traffic is well down — if email volume or frequency has been reduced, now is a good time to lean back in.
With returning customers making up just 36.5% of orders, there is an opportunity to build a stronger post-purchase sequence for the large volume of new customers acquired recently, particularly around the BFF+ which has no prior year comparison.
This week: Set up or review the post-purchase email flow for BFF+ buyers specifically, as 220 units were sold to a largely new customer base who have no prior relationship with the brand and are worth nurturing toward a second purchase.
