Analyst Notes
Ecommerce & Conversion
Working
Average order value is tracking above forecast at $145 versus the $140 target, suggesting customers who do visit are buying confidently and likely responding well to the current product mix led by the BFF+ and BFF.
Store sales had a standout week at $5,556 versus $1,305 last year, a 326% lift, which points to strong in-person engagement that is partially offsetting the online shortfall.
Needs Attention
The primary drag this week is a 43% drop in total sessions to 17,872, with nearly every channel down versus last year — organic search, direct, and email are all lower in absolute terms, meaning the sales decline is a traffic problem more than a conversion problem.
The return rate has jumped sharply to 7.9% (56 units, $7,036) from just 1.6% last year, which is worth investigating by product and size to understand whether fit, quality, or customer expectations are the driver.
This week: Pull a returns report by product and size this week to identify whether the spike is concentrated in one style — the BFF+ is the top seller with no last-year comparison, making it the most likely candidate to investigate first.
Paid Media
Working
Google Ads is performing well above its return on ad spend forecast of 3.0x, delivering 39.6x this week on $1,808 spend, with a cost per order of $4 against a $65 forecast — the Shopping and Performance Max campaigns are clearly punching above their weight.
Paid social is the single largest traffic driver this week at 5,931 sessions and 33% of the session mix, indicating Meta creative is pulling people to the site even without specific spend data reported.
Needs Attention
Total paid media spend is down 43% versus last year at $1,808, and while efficiency metrics look strong, the absolute volume of paid-driven orders will be limited at this investment level — the low spend may be a contributor to the overall session decline.
There is no Meta spend or performance data reported this week, which makes it difficult to assess whether paid social traffic is converting or simply browsing, and whether the channel is being actively managed.
This week: Confirm whether Meta campaigns are live and get spend and conversion data included in next week's report so paid social traffic can be properly evaluated against its contribution to revenue.
Retention & Revenue
Working
The Golden Ticket email leak sent on 17 June achieved a 63.9% open rate, which is an exceptionally strong result and suggests the audience found the subject line or preview text genuinely compelling.
New customer mix is sitting at 63.5%, close to the 65% forecast and well above last year's 47.7%, which points to the current campaign activity doing a good job of bringing in fresh buyers.
Needs Attention
Despite the strong open rates, the two Golden Ticket emails combined generated $5,777 in revenue against a backdrop of $71,640 in total online sales, meaning email is contributing around 8% of revenue — a relatively modest share given the audience engagement on display.
Protecty Stuff dropped sharply from 176 units last year to 36 units this week and carries 131 weeks of stock cover, which represents a significant overstock risk that retention campaigns could help address if a targeted offer or bundle was directed at existing customers.
This week: Build a targeted email this week to existing customers featuring Protecty Stuff as a add-on or bundle with the BFF+ or BFF, using the Golden Ticket momentum while the campaign is still front of mind.
