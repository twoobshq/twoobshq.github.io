Analyst Notes
Ecommerce & Conversion
Working
Conversion rate hit 3.08% against a 3.0% forecast, and online net sales are up 52.7% on last year despite sessions running 10.5% lower — the store is doing more with less traffic.
The store had a standout week at $4,708 in net sales, up 220% on last year, suggesting in-person momentum is building meaningfully.
Needs Attention
Returns have jumped to 6.8% of sales (44 units, $6,448) versus 4.3% last year, which is worth watching as average order value rises — higher-priced purchases may be driving more buyer hesitation or fit issues.
New customer mix at 60.2% is tracking below the 65% forecast, and with sessions down year-on-year, top-of-funnel traffic needs a closer look to ensure acquisition pace keeps up.
This week: Pull the return reason data for this week and check whether a specific product or size is driving the spike in returns before it compounds further.
Paid Media
Working
Paid media is delivering a return on ad spend of 5.7x at a cost per order of $26, against a forecast of 3.0x and $65 respectively — spend is down 31.6% on last year yet revenue is significantly higher, meaning efficiency has nearly doubled.
Paid Social is the largest traffic driver at 32% of sessions and Meta is doing the heavy lifting at $12,359 spend, suggesting current creative and audience targeting is resonating well.
Needs Attention
Google Ads paid search is generating just 36 sessions this week from $1,650 in spend spread across three campaigns, which is a very low volume relative to cost and worth auditing for wasted budget.
BFF and BFF+ Shopping campaigns are running separately from the main Shopping campaign, so it is worth confirming there is no audience overlap or keyword cannibalisation diluting efficiency across the three Google campaigns.
This week: Review the three Google Ads campaigns this week to consolidate or pause any overlap, and assess whether the $330 BFF and BFF+ Shopping campaign is incrementally adding orders or duplicating what the main Shopping campaign already captures.
Retention & Revenue
Working
The Natural Whine relaunch email pulled a 46.6% open rate and $6,764 in revenue from the subscriber segment, which is a strong result and signals the relaunch concept landed well with the existing customer base.
Average order value online is $152 against a $140 forecast and well above last year's $108, suggesting customers are either trading up to newer styles like the BFF+ or adding accessories to their orders.
Needs Attention
The public version of the Natural Whine relaunch email generated only $626 in revenue despite the same strong open rate, which points to a meaningful difference in purchase intent between the loyal subscriber base and the broader audience.
New customers represent 60.2% of orders this week, so roughly four in ten buyers are returning customers — it is worth checking whether any automated retention flows (win-back, post-purchase) are scheduled to follow up the relaunch campaign and convert the interest into repeat purchases.
This week: Schedule a post-purchase follow-up or cross-sell flow for anyone who bought during the Natural Whine relaunch this week, while the brand moment is still fresh.
